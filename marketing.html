Frequently Asked Questions

What age is this for?
NextGen School is designed specifically for children ages 9-13. Our courses are age-appropriate, engaging, and built to match the cognitive development of this age group. Younger kids (9-10) love the visual, game-like approach, while older kids (11-13) appreciate the deeper concepts.
Does my child need coding experience?
Absolutely not! Our entire platform is visual, drag-and-drop. No coding, no command lines, no technical prerequisites. If your child can use a tablet or computer, they can use NextGen School. We focus on AI THINKING skills, not programming.
How long are the courses?
Each course contains 20+ missions that take 15-30 minutes each. Kids can work at their own pace ‚Äî there's no rush. Most children complete a full course in 4-8 weeks depending on how often they practice.
Is this safe for kids?
Absolutely. Safety is our top priority. There's no social features, no chat, no external links within courses. All content is age-appropriate and reviewed by educators. Parents have full visibility into their child's activity through the progress dashboard.
Can I track my child's progress?
Yes! Every plan includes a parent dashboard where you can see which missions your child has completed, their scores, time spent learning, and skills developed. The Adventurer and Family plans include advanced analytics and milestone notifications.
What makes this different from other STEM programs?
Most programs teach kids to CODE. We teach kids to THINK. Before a child writes a single line of code, they need to understand how AI works, where it fails, and how to use it responsibly. That's what NextGen School provides ‚Äî the critical thinking foundation that makes every other skill more valuable.
